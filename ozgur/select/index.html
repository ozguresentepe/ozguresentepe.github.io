Woche 1

  • We started with an introduction. So, we had only 2 questions for the day.
  • The first question was about the existence of solutions to a linear Diophantine equation. Lena Felberfarmer volunteered for it. She gave us a necessary and sufficient condition.
  • For the second question Alexandra volunteered and showed us how to find the solution set to linear Diophantine equation in two variables. We found one particular solution and then added solutions to the homogeneous equations to it to get the general solution.
  • Fabian asked a clarifying question which resulted in a nice discussion about why Alexandra's method gives us solutions and also why it gives us all the solutions. Fabian's idea was to get help from the linear algebra/geometry intuition and see the solution set as a "line" which turned out to be a correct idea in this case. But in Diophantine equations our linear algebra intuition may fail us sometimes as we progress in the course, be careful!
  • I learned a joke about why people knock the desk at the end of a talk/lecture instead of clapping.
