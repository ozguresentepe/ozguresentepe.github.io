Auslander-Reiten annihilators

The Auslander-Reiten Conjecture for Gorenstein local rings

The statement.
Let $R$ be a Gorenstein local ring and $M$ a maximal Cohen-Macaulay $R$-module. If $\mathrm{Ext}_R^i(M, M) = 0$ for every $i \geq 1$, then $M$ is a free $R$-module.
The animation.
Identify the lattice point $(a,b)$ with the Ext module $\mathrm{Ext}_R^1(\Omega^a M, \Omega^b M)$ where $\Omega$ is the syzygy operator. This identifies the points $(i,0)$ on the $x$-axis with the self extensions $\mathrm{Ext}_R^1(\Omega^i M, M) = \mathrm{Ext}_R^{i+1}(M,M)$. In the beginning of the animation, points on the $x$-axis vanish, which tells us the vanishing of the self extensions. This is followed by the vanishing of $(0,1)$ which corresponds to $\mathrm{Ext}_R^1(M, \Omega M)$. This module vanishes if and only if $M$ is free. So when that vanishes, all Ext's vanish. The Conjecture states that if the $x$-axis vanish, then everything vanishes. So, if the bottom row collapses, then the whole lattice collapses. And in my paper, I studied the ideal of ring elements which ``annihilate the bottom row" and compared it with the ideal which ``annihilate the point $(0,1)$".
Notes.
(1) On the stable category of maximal Cohen-Macaulay modules over a Gorenstein local ring, the syzygy operator is an automorphism. This actually gives us an identification $(a,b) \sim (a+1, b+1)$ in our lattice.
(2) Instead of interpreting the animation as the vanishing of the Ext modules, we can interpet it as some ring element annihilating the Ext modules. This is the motivation behind my work on Auslander-Reiten annihilators.
(3) I proved that if the Krull dimension of $R$ is $d \geq 2$ and if $R$ is an isolated singularity, then the annihilator of $\mathrm{Ext}_R^1(M, \Omega M)$ is equal to the annihilator of $\mathrm{Ext}_R^{d-1}(M,M) = \mathrm{Ext}_R^1(\Omega^{d-2} M, M)$. If in the animation, we identify the lattice points whose corresponding Ext modules agree, this theorem identifies $(d-2,0)$ with $(0,1)$.
